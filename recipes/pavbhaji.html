Pav Bhaji

Description

Pav Bhaji is a fast food dish from India, consisting of a thick vegetable curry served with a soft bread roll. Its origins are in the state of Maharashtra. The dish originated in the 1850s as a fast lunchtime dish for textile mill workers in Mumbai. Pav bhaji was later served at restaurants throughout the city. Pav means bread roll. Bhaji in Marathi means vegetable dish. Pav bhaji consists of bhaji (a thick potato-based curry) garnished with coriander, chopped onion, and a dash of lemon and lightly toasted pav. The pav is usually buttered on all sides.

Ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Peas
  • Carrots
  • Capsicum
  • Butter
  • Herbs
  • Spices
  • Bread rolls

Instructions

  1. Boil potatoes, peas, and carrots in a pressure cooker.
  2. Mash the boiled vegetables and keep them aside.
  3. Heat butter in a pan and add chopped onions to it.
  4. Saute the onions until translucent.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, capsicum, herbs, and spices to the pan.
  6. Cook the vegetables until they are soft and mushy.
  7. Add the mashed vegetables to the pan and mix well.
  8. Cook the bhaji until it thickens.
  9. Toast the bread rolls with butter on a griddle.
  10. Serve the hot bhaji with buttered bread rolls, chopped onions, and a dash of lemon.
