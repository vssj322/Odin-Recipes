Dosa

Description

Dosa is a type of pancake from the Indian subcontinent, made from a fermented batter. It is somewhat similar to a crepe in appearance, although savoury flavours are generally emphasized. Its main ingredients are rice and black gram. Dosa is a typical part of the South Indian diet and popular all over the Indian subcontinent. Traditionally, Dosa is served hot along with sambar, a stuffing of potatoes, and chutney. It can be consumed with idli podi as well.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Black gram
  • Salt
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Soak rice and black gram in water for 5-6 hours.
  2. Grind the soaked rice and black gram to a fine paste.
  3. Add salt to the paste and let it ferment overnight.
  4. Heat a pan and pour a ladle of the batter on it.
  5. Spread the batter in a circular motion to make a thin pancake.
  6. Drizzle some oil on the edges and cook until golden brown.
  7. Flip the dosa and cook for another minute.
  8. Serve hot with sambar and chutney.
